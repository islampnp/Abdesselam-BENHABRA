Customer Preference Center (CPC)

The Customer Preference Center (CPC) is a critical project designed to empower healthcare professionals (HCPs) with control over their data and communication preferences. As [Your Position], I played a pivotal role in the development and implementation of this innovative platform.

  • Collaborated with cross-functional teams to ensure the CPC aligned with organizational goals and met the evolving needs of HCPs.

  • Enhanced user satisfaction: HCPs reported increased satisfaction with the CPC platform, citing its ease of use and the control it provided over their preferences.
  • Strengthened compliance: The CPC ensured compliance with data privacy regulations, mitigating risks and protecting the organization's reputation.

Implementing a CPC and establishing data synchronization is an investment that pays off. It builds customer trust, enhances marketing effectiveness, and provides data-driven insights for informed business decisions.

SCOPE - WFP Beneficiary Management

SCOPE is the World Food Programme's comprehensive management platform for beneficiary and transfer management, covering the entire program intervention cycle.

My mission involved developing the implementation plan in partnership with the program unit and WFP RBC Region, rolling out the project across five camps for 8,000 pregnant and lactating women. This included purchasing IT assets such as SCOPE printers, SCOPE cards, mPOS, and SAM cards, followed by scaling up the project in two camps through training sessions for WFP staff, retailers, and partners.

WFP SCOPE Login

WFP SCOPE Documentation

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Developed and implemented comprehensive plans for the SCOPE project in collaboration with WFP stakeholders.
  • Coordinated the procurement of necessary IT assets to ensure project readiness.
  • Conducted training sessions for WFP staff, retailers, and partners to ensure successful project implementation.
  • Monitored project progress and provided support to address any challenges.
  • Facilitated communication between various stakeholders to enhance project efficiency.

What is SCOPE used for?

SCOPE supports:

  • Registration and importing of household identities
  • Deduplication and management of personal data for eligible beneficiaries
  • Creation of beneficiary lists and transfer values
  • Assistance transfer (cash-based and in-kind)
  • Authentication and tracking of assistance delivery
  • Storage and management of operational data

WFP utilizes SCOPE to maintain a responsive information system for effective programming across various transfer modalities, enabling users to know beneficiaries, understand entitlements, issue instructions to partners, analyze feedback on transfers, and manage operational information throughout the intervention cycle. This ultimately enhances monitoring, risk control, and decision-making for improved targeting.

Network Monitoring and Anomaly Detection System

Developed a Django-based web application for network monitoring and analysis, utilizing TensorFlow to create a machine learning model for anomaly detection in network traffic, achieving an accuracy of 93%. This project aimed to build a scalable and efficient system for collecting and analyzing network traffic data.

The machine learning model was trained on a large dataset of network traffic, categorized as either normal or anomalous. By integrating this model into the web application, real-time anomaly detection was enabled, allowing for timely identification and reporting of potential threats.

This project holds significant potential for organizations seeking to enhance their network security. By promptly identifying anomalies, the system aids in preventing network outages and security breaches.
